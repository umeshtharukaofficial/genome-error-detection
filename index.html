Why Accuracy Isn't Enough

In genomics, errors are rare. A model that always guesses 'no error' could be 99% accurate but useless. We must focus on metrics that handle this class imbalance, like Precision and Recall.

Precision: The Quality of Predictions

Of all the times we predicted an error, how often were we right? High precision minimizes false alarms.

This chart illustrates precision as the ratio of True Positives to all positive predictions. It's a measure of exactness.

Recall: The Quantity of Detections

Of all the actual errors that exist, how many did we find? High recall minimizes missed errors.

Recall is visualized as the ratio of True Positives to all actual positives in the data. It's a measure of completeness.
